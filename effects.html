Positive Effects

  • Economic Growth: Countries can access international markets, attract foreign investment, and boost development.
  • Access to Technology: Global communication and information sharing speed up innovation and scientific progress.
  • Cultural Exchange: People are exposed to new languages, foods, music, and lifestyles, creating greater cultural understanding.
  • Improved Education and Healthcare: Knowledge sharing and funding help improve education systems and healthcare facilities worldwide.

    • Image: Positive Globalization affects — economically, culturally, and environmentally.

Negative Effects

  • Job Loss: Some local industries cannot compete with large international companies, leading to unemployment.
  • Environmental Damage: Increased industrial activity and global transport contribute to pollution and climate change.
  • Cultural Erosion: Traditional customs and languages can be lost due to the dominance of global (often Western) culture.
  • Inequality: Wealth is not distributed equally, and some countries or social groups benefit far more than others.
